Get a Transcript by Email

Standalone use case for Privacy & CELA sign-off

📄 What This Prototype Shows

A customer chatting with Ask Microsoft on microsoft.com can request a copy of their conversation transcript via email. This is a customer-initiated, transactional action — the customer chooses to receive a transcript, and we collect the minimum information needed to deliver it.

Key framing: This is a service we offer, not something we push. The customer is in control. Because they are requesting the transcript, the onus is on them to provide their details.

  • Step 1: Customer has a natural conversation with Ask Microsoft about M365 plans.
  • Step 2: At some point, we surface an option: “Would you like a copy of this conversation transcript?”
  • Step 3: Customer clicks yes → a collection card appears asking for first name and email, with a notice about what we’ll do with this information.
  • Step 4: Customer submits → confirmation. Transcript is emailed.
Trigger mechanism TBD: Whether the transcript offer is a button (like “Get a code”), an always-on link, or intent-based — that’s an internal decision. For this review, we’re showing it as a soft nudge to illustrate the flow. Privacy does not need to weigh in on trigger timing.

✅ What We Need Sign-Off On

1The use case itself
Collecting contact information (first name, email) in exchange for sending a conversation transcript. Is this permissible?
2The language on the collection card
The notice text reads: “We may save conversations to improve your experience and personalize future interactions with Microsoft. If you later sign in or share your email this conversation may be connected to your profile to provide more seamless and personalized experiences.” — Is this sufficient? What changes are needed?
3Sending the transcript as-is
For MVP, the customer receives the raw conversation transcript (not an AI summary). Are there PI concerns with sending the full transcript?
4Contact storage in UCMP
The contact details collected here will also be stored in the back end for potential future personalization. Are there additional requirements for this?
Note for reviewers: We’re keeping the trigger placement and timing as an internal product decision. We’d like your input focused on the use case, the wording, and the data handling. If there are concerns about data retention or purge, please flag those as well.

⚙ What Happens Behind the Scenes

  • Transcript delivery: The raw conversation transcript is emailed to the customer when the session ends.
  • Contact creation: First name and email are stored as a contact in UCMP for potential future outreach.
  • No promotional emails from this flow: Any future promotional outreach would follow existing CPM guidelines and rules independently. This flow does not set up or trigger promotional emails.
  • No AI summarization (MVP): Transcript is sent as-is. Summarization may be explored post-MVP pending further review.
Open items (not blocking this review): UCMP field requirements (pending Dayton alignment), ingestion validation rules, activity/interaction ID creation.
