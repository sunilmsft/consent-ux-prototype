1

Ask Microsoft Chat — Trigger & Contact Capture

MVP

What changes: The Ask Microsoft chat widget needs a trigger mechanism to present the transcript email offer, plus a collection card (reusing the Quoting Agent form component) for contact capture.

Important concept — two separate triggers per option:

Trigger 1 (Show the offer): When the customer is prompted to provide their email — this varies by option.
Trigger 2 (Send the email): When the transcript email is actually delivered — this is always session end (goodbye or idle timeout). The email captures the full conversation regardless of how many turns happen after the customer submits their info.

Trigger options:

TriggerTrigger 1: Show OfferTrigger 2: Send EmailProsCons
Turn-based
(after N turns)		 After ~3 customer messages, a non-blocking inline card appears Session end (goodbye or idle timeout) — full transcript Simple to implement; proven in prototype; non-intrusive Arbitrary timing — may miss short sessions or annoy on quick lookups
Intent-based
(customer asks)		 Customer says "email this", "send me a recap", or types "Email Summary" Session end (goodbye or idle timeout) — full transcript Customer-initiated = highest engagement; zero friction for those who don't want it Requires NLU/intent classification; most customers won't know the option exists
Always-on
(persistent CTA)		 Persistent "📧 Get transcript" button visible in chat header/footer at all times Session end (goodbye or idle timeout) — full transcript Always discoverable; no timing logic needed Uses screen real estate permanently; may feel "salesy"
Always-on: Footer
(placement variant)		 "Receive transcript" link in the footer area next to "AI generated content" disclaimer — currently unused white space Session end (goodbye or idle timeout) — full transcript Uses existing empty space; doesn't clutter the chat; unobtrusive Low visibility — customers may not notice it
Always-on: Kebab menu
(placement variant)		 "Receive transcript" option inside a ⋮ three-dot menu in the chat header Session end (goodbye or idle timeout) — full transcript Clean UI; scales as more features are added to the menu Lowest discoverability — hidden behind a menu tap; not ideal for MVP alone

MVP Recommendation: Turn-based (~3 turns) as the primary trigger + intent-based ("Email Summary") as a re-invoke mechanism. This is what the current prototype implements and balances discoverability with non-intrusiveness. The offer card is dismissible and chat always continues normally.

Collection card captures:

  • Required: First name, Last name, Email, Country, Company name
  • Optional: Job title, Company size
  • Dynamic consent: Country selection drives opt-out (US/non-GDPR) vs. opt-in (EU/GDPR) consent model via CPM
  • Component reuse: Reuses existing Quoting Agent form component — not a net-new build

Email validation & abuse prevention:

  • Format validation: Client-side + server-side regex check to ensure the email address is a valid format before submission
  • Disposable/temporary email blocking: Reject known throwaway email domains (mailinator, guerrillamail, etc.)
CELA / Privacy concern — email spoofing / abuse vector: What stops someone from entering another person's email address (e.g., a CEO, a competitor, or a random person) to spam them with unsolicited transcripts?

Mitigation options to evaluate:
  • Rate limiting: Cap the number of transcript emails that can be sent to the same email address within a time window (e.g., max 2 per 24 hours). Limits spam volume but doesn't prevent the first unwanted email.
  • One-click unsubscribe / block: Include an unsubscribe link in the transcript email so recipients can block future transcript sends to their address. Reactive rather than preventive.
  • Domain/VIP blocklist: Maintain a blocklist of high-profile domains or specific email addresses that cannot be targeted. Hard to maintain and doesn't scale.
  • Session-email correlation: Cross-reference session behavior patterns (e.g., if the same IP sends transcripts to 50 different emails, flag it).

MVP recommendation: Implement rate limiting + one-click unsubscribe at minimum. Long-term, authentication (user sign-in) will solve email ownership verification — OTP was considered and rejected due to the net-new capability build, security review overhead, and complexity.
Key UX principle: The transcript email is always sent regardless of marketing consent choice. The consent checkbox only governs whether Microsoft may send tailored recommendations (marketing activities via CPM/UCMP).

Transcript Save & Processing

MVP

What's built: When the customer submits the collection card, the system needs to persist the conversation and process it for email delivery.

  • Session tracking: Conversation turns are already captured in the chat backend — the transcript save hooks into this existing data
  • Email validation (server-side): Re-validate email format server-side before sending. Apply rate limiting (max sends per email per time window) and check against blocklists to prevent abuse
  • PII scrubbing: Before email delivery, PII that the customer may have typed in-chat (phone numbers, addresses, etc.) must be scrubbed from the transcript body
  • LLM summary generation: An AI-generated summary of the conversation is produced for the email body (key topics discussed, recommendations given, links shared)
  • Trigger for email send: Transcript email fires on session end — either graceful (customer says goodbye) or idle timeout (customer closes tab / goes silent)
Resolved (Srikar sync 5/15): Email will contain both — an LLM-generated summary at the top of the email body followed by the full raw transcript (PII scrubbed). Summary-first format sets up the inbound reply flow in Phase 2 (actionable items, next steps).

MVP → Phase 2

Why not URA for MVP? MVP only requires outbound email delivery. ACS is simpler, faster to ship, and already used in the Quotex flow. URA adds value only when we need to handle inbound customer replies — that's a Phase 2 capability.
MVP Recommendation (confirmed Srikar sync 5/15): Use ACS for outbound transcript email delivery (mirrors Quotex pattern). Claire confirmed the URA is being reworked (V-Next) — her recommendation is to keep MVP simple on ACS. Switching to URA in Phase 2 is a lightweight change (update the reply-to address to point to URA instead of unmonitored inbox).
Action item: Check with Claire on whether ACS has any built-in email validation or abuse prevention before sending (rate limiting, blocklist checks, etc.).

Email content (what the customer receives):

  • Subject: "Your Ask Microsoft conversation transcript" (or similar branded)
  • Body — Summary section: LLM-generated summary of key topics, recommendations, and links from the conversation
  • Body — Full transcript: Raw conversation transcript (PII scrubbed) — either inline as an expandable section or as an attachment
  • Branding: Microsoft branded HTML template with appropriate headers/footers
  • No marketing CTAs in MVP: Keep it clean — transcript delivery only. Cross-sell / recommended next steps can be a Phase 2 enhancement

UCMP — Contact Profile Create / Update (No Insights for MVP)

MVP

What's built: When the customer submits their contact info, we write to UCMP to create or update a contact profile. Contact creation/update only — no insights or marketing activity writes for MVP.

Scope narrowed (Srikar sync 5/15): MVP scope is send the email + create/update the contact in UCMP. That's it. Insights (marketing activities) are removed from MVP scope for the following reasons:
  • Authenticated user conflict: If a user is signed into the site AND provides a different email in chat, there's a conflict on which identity to associate insights with
  • Consent complexity: Insights require checking cookie banner + MSRs consent — contact creation does NOT (confirmed by privacy)
  • Permutation explosion: Factoring in authenticated vs. anonymous × cookie consent × MSRs consent × form consent creates too many edge cases for MVP
Privacy confirmed: Creating or updating a contact in UCMP does not require honoring cookie consent or MSRs controls. The consent captured on the form (opt-in/opt-out) is sufficient. This significantly simplifies the MVP flow.
  • New contact: If the email doesn't exist in UCMP, create a new contact profile with the captured fields (name, email, country, company, job title, company size)
  • Existing contact: If the email already exists in UCMP, update the profile with any new or missing fields (dedup by email)
  • Source attribution: Tag the contact as a web agent lead — lead source, site URL, session ID, timestamp. Note: Srikar confirmed source attribution does not currently exist in UCMP — needs validation with Dayton/UCMP team.
  • Transcript link: Optionally store a reference/link to the transcript for seller context when the lead is handed off
Open questions for UCMP:
  • What is the dedup strategy if the same email submits across multiple sessions? (Overwrite concern — can't distinguish new vs. update)
  • Does UCMP have a LeadSpace or ingestion layer that validates incoming contacts for bot/spam prevention?
  • What data retention / purge process applies? (GDPR Art. 17, CCPA)
  • If the customer already has a profile from the Quoting Agent flow, how do we merge?
  • Is source attribution feasible as a new field? Needs UCMP intake submission (this doc serves as the basis).
Seller handoff context: When a contact is created/updated in UCMP, the seller who picks up the lead should see: (1) the AI-generated conversation summary, (2) products/plans discussed, (3) customer's stated needs — giving them pre-qualified context instead of a cold call.

CPM — Consent Preference Management

MVP

What's built: A separate API call to CPM (Consent Preference Management) to store the customer's marketing consent preference. This is distinct from the UCMP contact write.

  • What gets sent: Email address + SBO topic + consent state (opted-in or opted-out)
  • New contacts: CPM can create a new entry even for net-new contacts — just needs email + topic + consent
  • Consent logic: Driven dynamically by country (non-GDPR = opt-out default, GDPR = opt-in default). The form captures the customer's choice and passes it to CPM.
  • Downstream impact: Once a contact has an opt-in state in CPM, marketing can send promotional emails (promotions, offers, etc.) until the customer opts out
Open question (Srikar sync 5/15): If a customer does NOT tick the opt-in checkbox, does that just mean "no action" (preserve any prior consent state), or does it actively opt them OUT of any pre-existing consent? This has materially different implications. Needs clarification with CPM team.
