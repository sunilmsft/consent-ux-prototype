Option A: Notice + Opt-Out — Preferred Consent Model ★

Conversation saved by default with upfront notice. Email capture mid-conversation enables identity connection — chat keeps going after. Summary auto-sent when customer leaves.

Low Friction

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-Out (Preferred)

  • Welcome notice: Consent notice embedded in the welcome message — conversation saved by default. Customer can [Opt out] anytime.
  • Mid-conversation nudge: After a few exchanges, agent offers to connect via email — framed as enablement ("keep this for later"), not a close.
  • Email = persistence toggle: Entering email connects identity to saved content. Chat continues seamlessly — no interruption.
  • Summary auto-sent: When the customer naturally leaves, summary is emailed automatically.
  • Returning user: "Continue a previous conversation?" — email lookup reconnects to prior session.
Key difference: Email is NOT a conversation-ending action. It's a mid-conversation enablement that unlocks persistence and continuity. The customer decides when the conversation is over.
If user opts out: Agent skips the email nudge entirely. Conversation flows without any email ask. If the customer later explicitly requests a summary (e.g., "email me a summary"), the email capture appears — honoring their intent.
Aligns with Preferred consent model: "We may save conversations to improve your experience..." Strongest balance of trust, usability, and data value. Default for most eCommerce agent experiences.

Option B: Notice + Opt-In Explicit Consent Before Saving

Nothing is saved unless the customer explicitly opts in. After a few exchanges, a professional consent prompt gives the customer full control.

Medium Friction

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-In (Model #3)

  • Welcome: Standard welcome with no consent notice. Nothing is saved by default.
  • After engagement: Once the customer has shown interest, a consent prompt asks: "Would you like to save this conversation?" with clear opt-in/decline.
  • If opted in: Agent offers email connection mid-conversation to enable continuity. Chat continues after email capture.
  • If declined: Conversation continues normally with no further prompts. Customer can still request a summary at any time.
  • Returning user: "Continue a previous conversation?" email lookup reconnects to prior session.
Key difference from Option A: No data is saved by default. The customer must actively choose to enable saving. This is the strongest model for Banner countries, sensitive markets, and early pilots.
If customer declines: No saving occurs. No email nudge is shown. But if the customer later explicitly asks (e.g., "Can you email me a summary?"), the request is honored.
Aligns with Opt-In consent model: "Would you like us to save this conversation to personalize future interactions?" Strongest privacy posture while still enabling data capture for willing customers.

Option C: End-of-Chat Summary — Capture at the Finish Line

Fully anonymous conversation. At the end, agent offers to email a summary — the most natural moment to capture identity.

Lowest Friction

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-In at Exit

  • Steps 1–3: Fully anonymous, no prompts.
  • Step 4: At wrap-up, agent offers a summary card.
  • Step 5: Email capture with continuity disclosure.
Privacy: Nothing stored until explicit opt-in at exit. Easiest to defend with CELA. Best for sensitive markets.

Option D: Always-On Passive Link Customer Controls the Moment

A persistent, non-intrusive link lives in the chat footer throughout the conversation. The customer taps it when they are ready no prompts, no banners, no interruptions.

Zero Push • Always Available

How it works

Consent: Always-On Passive via Persistent Footer Link

  • Welcome: Standard welcome. A subtle "Connect your email" link appears in the chat footer and stays visible throughout.
  • During conversation: No banners, no nudges, no prompts. The customer chats normally. The link is always there but never interrupts.
  • When ready: Customer taps the link at any point. An inline email capture expands. After connecting, the link updates to show connected status.
  • After connecting: Chat continues. Summary auto-sent when the customer leaves. Conversation is saved for continuity.
  • Returning user: "Continue a previous conversation?" email lookup reconnects to prior session.
Key difference from other options: No agent-initiated prompts at any point. The UI affordance is always visible but passive like a settings icon. The customer decides if, when, and whether to engage.
If customer never taps: Conversation proceeds fully anonymously. No data saved. No friction added.
Privacy: Strongest possible posture. No consent banner needed the action is the consent. Pairs well with A, B, or C as a passive complement.

Returning User Flow — Email-Based Reconnect

Every visitor sees the standard welcome. A subtle link lets returning users reconnect by entering their email. Works on any device — no cookie detection needed.

Addresses Privacy Blocker

How it works

Key insight

This flow only works when the previous session captured an email. If the user browsed anonymously, every visit is a fresh start — there's nothing to reconnect to.

  • Step 1: Standard welcome — same for everyone, new or returning.
  • Step 2: Subtle link below the welcome: "Have a saved conversation? Enter your email to continue."
  • Step 3: Customer enters email → system checks for a match.
  • Step 4 (match): Previous topic context loads. Conversation resumes.
  • Step 4 (no match): "No saved session found." Clean start.
Why this is better: No cookie detection, no private-vs-shared machine problem, no "Welcome back" assumption. Email is the key — works on any device, any browser. Solves Scenario 5 without needing device signals.
Privacy safe: Zero PII exposed until email is entered and matched. A stranger sees nothing. No topic hints, no names, no history — just the standard welcome + a generic reconnect link.
Auto-expire: Saved sessions expire after 48 hrs (configurable). Even matched sessions don't persist indefinitely.
Limitation: If the previous user never provided an email (e.g., Options C/D where email is end-of-chat or customer-initiated), there's nothing to reconnect to. This flow is only possible when email was captured in a prior session.
