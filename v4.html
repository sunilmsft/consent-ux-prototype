Option A: Notice + Opt-Out — Preferred Consent Model ★

Conversation saved by default with upfront notice. Email capture mid-conversation enables identity connection — chat keeps going after. Summary auto-sent when customer leaves.

Low Friction

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-Out (Preferred)

  • Welcome notice: Consent notice embedded in the welcome message — conversation saved by default. Customer can [Opt out] anytime.
  • Mid-conversation nudge: After a few exchanges, agent offers to connect via email — framed as enablement ("keep this for later"), not a close.
  • Email = persistence toggle: Entering email connects identity to saved content. Chat continues seamlessly — no interruption.
  • Summary auto-sent: When the customer naturally leaves, summary is emailed automatically.
  • Returning user: "Continue a previous conversation?" — email lookup reconnects to prior session.
Key difference: Email is NOT a conversation-ending action. It's a mid-conversation enablement that unlocks persistence and continuity. The customer decides when the conversation is over.
If user opts out: Agent skips the email nudge entirely. Conversation flows without any email ask. If the customer later explicitly requests a summary (e.g., "email me a summary"), the email capture appears — honoring their intent.
Aligns with Preferred consent model: "We may save conversations to improve your experience..." Strongest balance of trust, usability, and data value. Default for most eCommerce agent experiences.

Option B: Contextual Nudge — Subtle Banner After Engagement

Option D: Customer-Initiated — "Save My Chat"

The agent drops a subtle hint early in the conversation. When the customer is ready, they type "save my chat" (or tap a chip) to trigger the email capture. Full control with the customer.

Zero Push • Customer Controlled

How it works

Consent: Customer-Initiated Opt-In

  • Step 1: Standard welcome with a one-line hint: "Tip: Type 'save my chat' anytime to keep a copy."
  • Step 2–3: Normal conversation. No banners, no nudges.
  • Step 4: When the customer types "save my chat," the agent presents the email capture.
  • Step 5: Email captured with continuity disclosure. Done.
Why this works: Zero interruption. The customer decides if and when they want to save. The hint is ambient — easy to ignore if not interested, easy to act on when ready.
Privacy: Strongest possible posture. Nothing happens until the customer explicitly requests it. No consent banner needed — the action is the consent.

Returning User Flow — Email-Based Reconnect

Every visitor sees the standard welcome. A subtle link lets returning users reconnect by entering their email. Works on any device — no cookie detection needed.

Addresses Privacy Blocker

How it works

Key insight

This flow only works when the previous session captured an email. If the user browsed anonymously, every visit is a fresh start — there's nothing to reconnect to.

  • Step 1: Standard welcome — same for everyone, new or returning.
  • Step 2: Subtle link below the welcome: "Have a saved conversation? Enter your email to continue."
  • Step 3: Customer enters email → system checks for a match.
  • Step 4 (match): Previous topic context loads. Conversation resumes.
  • Step 4 (no match): "No saved session found." Clean start.
Why this is better: No cookie detection, no private-vs-shared machine problem, no "Welcome back" assumption. Email is the key — works on any device, any browser. Solves Scenario 5 without needing device signals.
Privacy safe: Zero PII exposed until email is entered and matched. A stranger sees nothing. No topic hints, no names, no history — just the standard welcome + a generic reconnect link.
Auto-expire: Saved sessions expire after 48 hrs (configurable). Even matched sessions don't persist indefinitely.
Limitation: If the previous user never provided an email (e.g., Options C/D where email is end-of-chat or customer-initiated), there's nothing to reconnect to. This flow is only possible when email was captured in a prior session.
