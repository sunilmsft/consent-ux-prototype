When a customer visits microsoft.com to explore Microsoft 365, they can chat with Ask Microsoft to compare plans, get answers, and connect with sales. These four options define how we transition that customer from anonymous to known — respectfully, transparently, and on their terms.

Each swim lane below shows the same customer journey on microsoft.com. The dashed circle marks where each option’s consent moment happens — always optional, never gated. The chat always continues regardless. Click any lane to explore its full walk-through.

Option A: Notice + Opt-Out — Preferred Consent Model ★

Conversation saved by default with upfront notice. Email capture mid-conversation enables identity connection — chat keeps going after. Summary auto-sent when customer leaves.

Low Friction

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-Out (Preferred)

  • Welcome notice: Consent notice embedded in the welcome message — conversation saved by default. Customer can [Opt out] anytime.
  • Returning customer link: A subtle “Returning?” link below the greeting lets returning customers self-identify and enter their email to reconnect. First-timers skip right past it.
  • Mid-conversation nudge: After a few exchanges, agent offers to connect via email — framed as enablement ("keep this for later"), not a close.
  • Email = persistence toggle: Entering email connects identity to saved content. Chat continues seamlessly — no interruption.
  • Summary auto-sent: When the customer naturally leaves, summary is emailed automatically.
  • Returning user: Same welcome screen — customer clicks the “Returning?” link to reconnect. No separate flow needed.
Key difference: Email is NOT a conversation-ending action. It's a mid-conversation enablement that unlocks persistence and continuity. The customer decides when the conversation is over.
If user opts out: Conversation flows naturally without any email nudge or capture. The agent simply continues the conversation.
One welcome, all customers: The “Returning?” link handles both new and returning customers in a single unified experience — no detection logic, no cookies, no separate flow.

Option B: Notice + Opt-In — Explicit Consent Model

Nothing saved unless you opt in. Email is optional. The customer decides what to save and whether to connect their email — both are independent choices.

Medium Friction

How it works

Consent: Explicit Opt-In · Banner countries

  • Welcome: Chat opens with opt-in consent: “We can save this conversation to improve your experience.” Customer clicks [Yes, save and personalize] or [No, continue without saving].
  • If opted in: Conversation is saved. Agent may later offer to connect email for a summary and return access — email is optional.
  • If email connected: Summary emailed at wrap-up. Customer can return and pick up where they left off.
  • If declined: Conversation continues normally. No data saved. Customer can still explicitly request a summary later.
  • Returning user: If email was provided, can resume previous conversation or start fresh.
Key difference from Option A: No data is saved by default. The customer must actively choose to enable saving in the welcome message. Saving and email are independent — you can save without connecting email.
If customer declines: No saving occurs. No email nudge is shown. But if the customer later explicitly asks (e.g., “Can you email me a summary?”), the request is honored.
Aligns with Opt-In consent model: Strongest privacy posture while still enabling data capture for willing customers. Default for Banner countries and sensitive markets.

Option C: Notice + End-of-Chat Summary — Summary at the Finish Line

Conversation saved by default with upfront notice (same as A). The difference: email capture happens at the end when the agent offers to send a summary — not mid-conversation.

End-of-Chat

How it works

Consent: Notice + Opt-Out (Summary at Exit)

  • Welcome notice: Consent notice embedded in the welcome message — conversation saved by default. Customer can [Opt out] anytime.
  • Returning customer link: A subtle “Returning?” pill below the greeting lets returning customers self-identify and enter their email to reconnect.
  • Uninterrupted conversation: No mid-conversation nudge or email prompt. The entire chat flows naturally without any identity capture.
  • End-of-chat summary: When the customer wraps up, the agent offers to email a summary of the conversation. Customer chooses “Yes, email me” or “No thanks.”
  • Email = persistence toggle: If they opt for the summary, email capture connects their identity. Summary is sent and conversation is saved for future return.
  • Returning user: Same welcome screen — customer clicks the “Returning?” pill to reconnect. No separate flow needed.
Key difference from A: Email is captured at the END when offering a summary, not mid-conversation. This means zero interruption during the active conversation — the customer gets full value first.
If user opts out: No summary offer at the end. Conversation flows and ends naturally without any email prompt.
One welcome, all customers: The “Returning?” link handles both new and returning customers in a single unified experience — same as Option A.

Option D: Always-On Passive — Customer Controls the Moment

A subtle "Save chat" button sits in the chat footer throughout the conversation. No prompts, no banners, no nudges. The customer taps it when ready — or not at all.

Zero Push • Always Available

How it works

Consent: Always-On Passive via Footer Button

  • Welcome: Standard welcome. A subtle "Save chat" button appears in the footer and stays throughout. No consent notice, no mention of saving.
  • During conversation: Zero prompts, zero nudges, zero banners. Customer chats normally. The button is there but never calls attention to itself.
  • When ready: Customer taps the button. Email capture appears inline. After connecting, button updates to "✓ Saved."
  • After connecting: Chat continues seamlessly. Summary auto-sent when customer leaves.
  • If customer never taps: Nothing happens. Conversation proceeds fully anonymously. No data saved. No friction.
  • Returning user: Same welcome screen with "Returning?" link — email lookup reconnects to prior session.
Key difference: No agent-initiated anything. The button is always there like a settings icon — passive, subtle, customer-driven. The action is the consent.
Privacy: Strongest possible posture. No consent banner needed. Pairs well with A, B, or C as a passive complement.
