Hi Alex,

To get desktop apps, Teams, scheduling tools, and optional premium AI features, the best fit is usually Microsoft 365 Business Standard or Business Premium with Microsoft 365 Copilot added for selected users.

Business Standard — Best core fit for managers
  • Includes: desktop, web, and mobile Office apps plus Teams
  • Scheduling: Microsoft Bookings for appointments and simple scheduling workflows
  • Collaboration: Outlook, OneDrive, Teams meetings, shared files, and day-to-day productivity
  • Good for: store managers and back-office roles that need full apps without advanced security requirements
Business Premium — Best when you also need stronger security
  • Everything in Standard + Intune device management and Microsoft Defender for Business
  • Security: Conditional Access, MFA enforcement, and stronger protection for shared/store devices
  • Good for: owner, IT, operations, finance, or any role handling sensitive information
AI add-on option — Microsoft 365 Copilot
  • Add Copilot for users who need help with reports, email drafting, meeting recaps, and content creation
  • Typical starting point: manager, analyst, or owner seats rather than every frontline user

Recommended path: Use Business Standard for manager productivity, Business Premium where you need stronger protection, and add Copilot selectively. For frontline associates, many teams still keep Business Basic to control cost.
